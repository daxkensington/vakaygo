## 1. Executive Summary

VakayGo is a live, Caribbean-born travel platform that unifies all six pillars of trip planning — Stays, Tours & Excursions, Dining, Events, Transport, and Local Guides — into a single destination. The platform is already operational at vakaygo.com with 5,050 pre-populated listings across 21 Caribbean islands, 21,146 photos, and a fully built technology stack supporting bookings, operator dashboards, messaging, and automated outreach.

Caribbean tourism generates $60-70 billion annually from 31.5 million stayover arrivals, yet no dominant Caribbean-specific platform exists. Travelers currently juggle 8-12 apps per trip, and 67% wish they could plan everything in one place. Meanwhile, local operators — the backbone of Caribbean tourism — surrender 15-30% of revenue to global platforms that treat the region as an afterthought.

VakayGo charges the lowest commissions in the industry: a combined 9% take rate (6% traveler fee + 3% operator commission), compared to 20-30% at Viator and 14-25% at Airbnb and Booking.com. This pricing, combined with deep local knowledge and a pre-built directory operators can claim instantly, positions VakayGo as the first platform that genuinely serves Caribbean tourism from the inside out.

The company is founded in Grenada by a team with direct tourism operations experience (Landfall Excursions), ensuring boots-on-the-ground market knowledge. Revenue is projected to reach $150K-250K in Year 1 (Grenada focus), scaling to $20M+ by Year 5 through systematic island-by-island expansion.

## 3. Problem Statement

### The Traveler Problem

Caribbean travelers face a fragmented planning experience. The average traveler uses **8-12 different apps and websites** to plan a single trip — one for flights, another for hotels, a third for tours, separate searches for restaurants and transport. This fragmentation leads to:

- **Wasted time:** Hours spent cross-referencing listings across platforms
- **Missed experiences:** Local gems invisible on global platforms
- **Overpayment:** Hidden fees stacked across multiple intermediaries
- **Decision fatigue:** 67% of travelers wish they could plan everything in one place
- **Trust gaps:** No unified reviews or quality signals across categories

### The Operator Problem

Caribbean tourism operators — tour guides, boutique hotels, restaurants, transport providers — face an equally broken system:

- **Excessive commissions:** Viator takes 20-30%, Airbnb takes 14-15%, Booking.com takes 15-25%
- **Platform neglect:** Global OTAs treat the Caribbean as a secondary market
- **Fragmented presence:** Operators must manage profiles on 5+ platforms
- **Limited technology:** Many operators still rely on WhatsApp, phone calls, and paper calendars
- **No unified dashboard:** Revenue, bookings, and guest communication scattered across tools

### The Market Gap

Despite being a $60-70 billion annual market, the Caribbean has **no dominant regional travel platform**. Global players (Airbnb, Viator, Booking.com, Expedia) offer limited Caribbean-specific features, incomplete coverage of local operators, and extract outsized commissions. This gap is VakayGo's opportunity.

## 6. Business Model & Revenue Streams

VakayGo employs a diversified revenue model with five distinct streams:

### Stream 1: Transaction Fees (Primary)

| Component | Rate | Paid By |
|---|---|---|
| Traveler service fee | 6% | Guest |
| Operator commission | 3% | Operator |
| **Total take rate** | **9%** | **Split** |

**Unit Economics Example:**
- Average booking value: $150
- Traveler pays: $159 ($150 + $9 service fee)
- Operator receives: $145.50 ($150 - $4.50 commission)
- VakayGo revenue: $13.50 per transaction

At 1,000 bookings/month (Year 2 target): **$162,000 annualized from transactions alone**

### Stream 2: Trip Insurance

- Optional add-on: 8% of booking value
- VakayGo retains 50% of insurance premium (4% effective margin)
- Estimated attachment rate: 15-25% of bookings

**Example:** On a $150 booking, insurance = $12. VakayGo keeps $6.

## 8. Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Grenada (Months 1-6)

Grenada is the beachhead market for three reasons:

1. **Founder presence** — Active tourism business (Landfall Excursions) on the ground
2. **Manageable scale** — ~200K stayover visitors/year, tight-knit operator community
3. **Government alignment** — Tourism = 60% of GDP; authorities incentivized to support local platforms

**Tactics:**
- Direct operator outreach via existing relationships
- Email outreach system (already built) to contact all Grenada listings
- Partnership with Grenada Tourism Authority
- On-the-ground demos at operator businesses
- Cruise port presence for traveler acquisition

### Phase 2: Eastern Caribbean Expansion (Months 6-18)

Expand to neighboring islands with similar operator profiles:
- St. Vincent & the Grenadines, Barbados, St. Lucia, Dominica, Trinidad & Tobago, Antigua & Barbuda

**Tactics:**
- Replicate Grenada playbook with local ambassadors
- Leverage cross-island traveler traffic (multi-island trips)
- Tourism board partnerships
- Digital marketing targeting island-specific travel searches

### Phase 3: Full Caribbean Coverage (Months 18-36)

Scale to all 21+ covered islands including Jamaica, Dominican Republic, Bahamas, Puerto Rico, and Cayman Islands.

### Phase 4: Beyond Caribbean (Year 4+)

Expand the model to adjacent tropical/island markets:
- Pacific Islands (Fiji, Tahiti, Bali)
- Indian Ocean (Maldives, Seychelles, Mauritius)
- Mediterranean islands (Santorini, Mallorca, Malta)
